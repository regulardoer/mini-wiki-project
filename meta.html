About Meta

Meta Platforms Inc., formerly known as Facebook, was founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg and fellow Harvard students. What began as a social networking website rapidly evolved into one of the most influential digital corporations in the world.

In 2021, the company rebranded itself as Meta to emphasize its strategic focus on building the "metaverse" â€” a digital ecosystem combining virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and immersive social experiences.

Today, Meta owns and operates globally dominant platforms including Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger, collectively serving billions of active users.

Digital Influence and Societal Impact

Meta generates the majority of its revenue through digital advertising, using advanced data analytics and algorithm-driven content delivery systems. Its platforms influence global communication patterns, public opinion, and digital culture.

Through algorithm-based news feeds and recommendation systems, Meta plays a central role in shaping information visibility and online engagement behaviors.

However, the company has faced substantial scrutiny regarding misinformation, political content moderation, data privacy concerns, and the psychological effects of social media usage.

These challenges highlight the broader debate surrounding the power and responsibility of large-scale technology platforms in modern society.
