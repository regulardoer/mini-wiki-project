About Google

Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin with the mission to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful.

Over the years, Google has evolved from a simple search engine into a global technology leader operating in fields such as digital advertising, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and mobile operating systems.

As a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc., Google plays a central role in shaping internet infrastructure, digital markets, and information accessibility worldwide.

Main Products and Services

  1. Google Search: The world's most widely used search engine.
  2. YouTube: A global video-sharing and streaming platform.
  3. Gmail: A widely used email service.
  4. Google Maps: Navigation and location-based services.
  5. Android: The world's most popular mobile operating system.

Through these services, Google reaches billions of users daily, creating a digital ecosystem that integrates communication, entertainment, productivity, and commerce.

Influence and Global Impact

Google dominates the global search engine market, controlling a significant percentage of worldwide search traffic. Its advertising model generates the majority of its revenue, making it one of the most profitable digital corporations in history.

The company's investments in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data analytics position it as a leading innovator in emerging technologies.

However, Google's influence also raises concerns regarding data privacy, algorithm transparency, and market competition, prompting regulatory scrutiny in multiple countries.
